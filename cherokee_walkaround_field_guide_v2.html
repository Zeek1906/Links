2
Hood / Engine Bay
Hood open, standing driver's side
▼
The Engine Story — Preempt the Objection First
"Under the hood is a 1.6-liter turbocharged hybrid — and before anybody says 'that sounds small for a Jeep,' let me reframe that. This is a third-generation EP6 engine, known as the Prince engine, originally developed in Europe and proven across millions of miles on Peugeot, Citroën and other Stellantis brands worldwide. It has won eight International Engine of the Year awards in its category. This is not a new experiment — it's a globally proven platform, localized for the Cherokee."
What Makes This Engine Different
  • All-aluminum construction — reduces weight without sacrificing durability
  • Variable-geometry turbine turbo — significantly reduces turbo lag; big deal for customers who've owned turbos before
  • Central Direct Injection (CDI) — fuel injected directly into the combustion chamber for more power and better efficiency
  • Hybrid Stop Start technology — shuts engine at stops to save fuel, restarts instantly via Motor 1
The Hybrid System — Two Motors, One Battery
"This isn't just a gasoline engine with a small battery assist. There are two electric motor generators integrated into the transmission working alongside the engine. Motor 1 starts the engine and keeps the battery charged — it also harvests energy from the engine any time there's surplus power. Motor 2 actually helps drive the wheels and captures energy every time you lift off the gas or press the brake. The system is constantly recovering energy from two different sources at the same time."
Key clarification for consultants
Regenerative braking kicks in two ways: when you apply the brake AND when you simply lift off the gas. Customers assume you have to press the brake pedal — correct this proactively. "You don't even have to press the brake. The moment you ease off the accelerator, the system starts recovering energy."
Battery + Liquid Cooling
"The 1.03 kWh high-voltage battery is liquid cooled — most competitors are still running air-cooled systems. Liquid cooling means consistent performance in summer heat or hard driving, and significantly longer battery life over time. And you never plug it in — ever."
Power Numbers
"Combined output is 210 horsepower and 230 lb-ft of torque. That torque hits immediately — the electric motor eliminates the hesitation you feel in a traditional turbo engine. No waiting to merge. No lag off the line."

Transmission — Address the CVT Question
"Unlike many hybrid competitors that use a belt-and-pulley CVT, the Cherokee uses a planetary gearset transmission. It drives like a conventional automatic — familiar, linear feel. No CVT drone, no rubberbanding. Customers who've hated CVTs will notice the difference on the test drive."
Competitive context
RAV4 Hybrid and CR-V Hybrid use e-CVT systems. Cherokee's planetary gearset is a meaningful technology differentiation — worth naming if a customer has driven either competitor.
"That engine sounds small for a Jeep."
"Third-generation EP6 — eight Engine of the Year awards, millions of miles proven globally. 210 horsepower, 230 lb-ft of torque hitting instantly because the electric motor eliminates lag. It outperforms what most customers expect the moment they drive it."
"Do I have to plug it in?"
"Never. The system charges itself two ways — every time you lift off the gas and every time you brake. Park anywhere, garage anywhere. Zero charging infrastructure needed."
"What about long-term reliability?"
"Third-generation engine with millions of miles of real-world proof across Europe. Liquid-cooled battery — most competitors use air cooling. This platform was engineered for longevity, not just launch specs."
So What
Preempt the "small engine" objection here before it reaches the desk. Third-gen, globally proven, liquid-cooled, planetary gearset — those four points close the anxiety loop. Don't leave this stop without addressing the CVT question proactively.
Trainer note: Watch for consultants who skip the Motor 1 / Motor 2 explanation. That's the most memorable powertrain story in the segment — coach them to use it. The "two energy recovery sources" framing is a genuine differentiator customers can repeat to their spouse.

Driver's Side Profile
Standing mid-vehicle, driver's side
▼
Size Story — Conquest Stop
"Step back and look at the profile. This Cherokee is about 188 inches long — and that extra length over competitors all went into the wheelbase. That's the number that creates rear legroom. You'll feel that when we get inside."
Ground Clearance + Approach Angle
"Notice the ride height — 8.1 inches of ground clearance. But the number that really tells the capability story is the approach angle: 19.2 degrees. That's the steepest incline the front of this vehicle can climb without the bumper making contact with the ground."
For new consultants — explain the angles
Approach angle is how steep of an incline the front of the vehicle can climb without scraping. Departure angle (at the rear) is the same concept leaving an obstacle. Cherokee's 19.2° approach and 31.4° departure are best-in-class — it can handle terrain the RAV4 and CR-V physically cannot. For customers who never plan to off-road: "Those numbers tell you this vehicle was engineered with real capability — not just designed to look rugged."
Ramp Breakover Angle

The ramp breakover angle is 19.4° — this is the measurement of how high of a crest or ridge the vehicle can clear without the underside making contact. Three angles working together: approach, breakover, departure. Cherokee's geometry is class-leading across all three.

Water Fording
"Cherokee can ford up to 20 inches of water at 5 mph. For context, that's deeper than most low-water crossings on popular trail systems. This is a legitimate go-anywhere vehicle."
Selec-Terrain — 4 Modes
"That toggle dial you'll see inside is the Selec-Terrain system — four modes: Auto, Sport, Snow, and Sand/Mud. Each one adjusts throttle response, transmission behavior, and traction control calibration for the conditions. The system does the thinking so the driver doesn't have to. Auto is the default for everyday driving — Snow and Sand/Mud are there when you need them."
Jeep Active Drive I 4x4
Front-wheel drive is the default for fuel efficiency. The rear axle engages automatically when traction is needed via the automatic 1-speed PTU (power transfer unit). Fully seamless — no driver input required. Also features a rear axle disconnect that re-engages instantly when conditions call for it, helping maintain balanced torque distribution and brake traction control.
Wheels + Body Details
"On the Overland you're getting 20-inch two-tone machine-finished alloy wheels. Notice the lower moldings and wheel arch flares are body-color — not matte plastic like you'd see on base trims. That's the detail that separates this truck visually from 40 feet away."

Competitive context
RAV4 Hybrid and CR-V Hybrid are not designed for trail use — their approach and departure angles don't come close. Even for customers who never off-road, this is a confidence play: Cherokee goes places the competition can't.
So What
This is your capability conquest stop. Size, clearance, approach/departure angles, water fording, and Selec-Terrain — this is where you earn the "true Jeep" credibility for a vehicle that's also a hybrid. Don't skip the angle explanation for new consultants or customers.
Trainer note: New consultants will skip the angle numbers — they don't know what they mean. Coach them to physically point to where the measurement is taken on the vehicle. Showing beats saying.

Rear / Cargo
Standing at the rear, liftgate open
▼
Departure Angle — Callback from Stop 3
"Before we open the liftgate — look at the rear geometry. That 31.4-degree departure angle means this vehicle can clear a drop-off on exit that most compact SUVs would scrape on. And that full-size spare under the floor? Most hybrid competitors sacrifice the spare for battery packaging. Cherokee kept it."
Taillights
"Rectangular LED taillights — clean, modern, high-visibility. The LED array is part of the same premium lighting package running front to back."
Liftgate
"Power liftgate with a hands-free kick sensor — when your hands are full, kick under the bumper and it opens. On the Overland, the liftgate height is adjustable so it clears a low garage ceiling. That 3-second delay before close keeps people from getting caught."
Trim note — power liftgate availability
Power liftgate with adjustable height is standard on Limited with Tech Group and Overland. The foot-activated version is standard on Overland. Use it as a demonstration moment — ask the customer about their lifestyle before you demo it.
Cargo Space
"33.6 cubic feet with seats up. 68.3 cubic feet with the second row folded flat. That's about a 30 percent improvement over the previous generation. And the load floor is completely flat — no hump, no awkward step loading heavy items."
Towing
"Standard tow rating is 3,500 pounds — best-in-class for compact hybrids. That's enough for a small boat, jet ski trailer, a camper, or a couple of ATVs. The Trailer Tow Group adds the hitch receiver, wiring harness, and trailer sway control — that's what unlocks the full 3,500 lb capacity."
Trailer Tow Group — what's included
Class III receiver hitch, 7 and 4 pin wiring harness, Trailer Hitch View with zoom, Trailer Blind Spot Detection, and compact spare. Available on all trims except Sport.
Safety Features at Rear Position
"Cherokee offers 140 standard and available safety and security features. A few worth calling out here: ParkView rear backup camera, Rear Park Assist, Rear Cross-Path Detection, and Blind Spot Monitoring with Turn Signal activation. The full safety suite is one of the most comprehensive in this segment."

Competitive context
RAV4 Hybrid tops out around 1,750 lbs towing. CR-V Hybrid is similarly limited. Cherokee more than doubles the segment average — and keeps the full-size spare that most hybrid competitors sacrifice for battery packaging.
"Does it really tow enough for a boat/camper?"
"3,500 pounds covers a small boat, jet ski trailer, teardrop camper, or two ATVs. If they need more, it's worth a conversation about a Grand Cherokee — but for most weekend use cases, Cherokee handles it."
So What
Full-size spare plus best-in-class towing is a one-two punch no compact hybrid competitor can match. This stop wins the utility argument. Don't rush it — and always ask what they tow before you quote the number.
Trainer note: The foot-activated liftgate is a high-impact demo moment. Coach consultants to ask a lifestyle question first — "Do you do a lot of grocery runs? Hardware store trips? Travel sports?" — then demonstrate it. Engagement questions make the feature personal.

Rear Seat
Rear passenger door open, customer seated
▼
Put Them in the Seat
"Go ahead and sit back. Notice the legroom. This is where that extra wheelbase actually lives — it all came here. If you've sat in the back of a RAV4 on a longer drive and felt cramped, you're going to notice the difference immediately."
Class-exclusive rear hip room
Cherokee offers unsurpassed rear hip room in its class — this is a class-exclusive claim. Shoulder-to-shoulder comfort for three adults in back is genuinely better than both RAV4 and CR-V. When customers have families, this closes.
Materials — Overland
"The Overland is upholstered in Capri faux leatherette with honeycomb perforations and contrast stitching. It wipes clean, it's durable, and frankly it's getting hard to tell from real leather unless someone points it out. Jeep made this call for sustainability and durability — right choice for families."
Rear Climate + Connectivity
  • Rear climate vents — second-row passengers control their own airflow
  • USB-C ports throughout the cabin — rear passengers stay charged
  • Second-row USB ports standard on Laredo and above
Cabin Quiet — Undersold Feature
"One thing worth mentioning before the demo drive: this cabin is significantly quieter than the outgoing model. Acoustic laminated windshield filters wind noise. Active Noise Cancellation uses the audio system to offset engine and exhaust frequencies — works even when the radio is off. And the Acoustic Vehicle Alerting System alerts pedestrians outside while staying nearly silent inside. Set that expectation now — customers notice it the moment they pull out of the lot."
Panoramic Sunroof
"That glass overhead is the panoramic sunroof — it nearly doubles the open feel of the cabin. On the Overland it's standard. From the rear seat it's especially noticeable — the cabin feels much larger than the footprint suggests."
So What
Put the customer in the seat and let them feel it. Unsurpassed rear hip room, quiet cabin, USB-C connectivity — this stop is where the size story becomes personal. Thirty seconds of silence after they sit is worth more than two minutes of talking.
Trainer note: New consultants rush this stop. Coach them to ask the customer to sit, then stop talking for a beat. Let the rear seat sell itself before jumping to the next feature.

Driver's Seat / Tech Stack
Customer in driver's seat — close stop
▼
Steering Wheel + Cluster
"First thing you'll feel is the steering wheel — performance-inspired design with a premium leather wrap. The flat top and bottom give you a clean line of sight straight into the 10.25-inch fully digital instrument cluster. Speed, navigation, and the hybrid power flow display are all right in your eyeline."
Hybrid power flow display
The digital cluster includes a real-time hybrid power flow display — shows customers visually when the electric motors are working, when the engine is charging the battery, and when regenerative braking is recovering energy. Demo this during the test drive. It makes the powertrain story tangible.
Uconnect 5 — 12.3-inch
"The 12.3-inch Uconnect 5 screen has wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, Alexa built in, and over-the-air update capability. Notice the physical hard buttons for defrost and AC — Jeep brought those back because they listened. Nobody wants to hunt through a touchscreen to defrost a windshield at 6am."
Rotary Shifter + Wireless Charging
"The rotary gear selector on the console — tactile, premium feel. Wireless charging pad with pressure tongs to keep the phone from sliding during turns. Small thing, meaningful on a daily basis."
Active Driving Assist — Level 2 Standard
"Active Driving Assist is Level 2 — adaptive cruise control combined with lane centering — standard across all trims. Hands-on, eyes-on, but it handles micro-corrections on the highway. On a RAV4 you're often paying extra for this. Here it comes on every Cherokee off the truck."
Level 2 vs Level 2+ clarification
Level 2 Active Driver Assist is standard across all trims — assists with steering and speed but driver must remain engaged. Level 2+ Hands-Free Active Driver Assist is available at higher trim or package level. Know the difference — customers will ask.

"How does this compare to the RAV4 Hybrid?"
"RAV4 Hybrid is a strong vehicle — but Cherokee has best-in-class approach and departure angles, more than double the towing capacity, a planetary gearset instead of CVT, Level 2 ADAS standard on every trim, and unsurpassed rear hip room.
