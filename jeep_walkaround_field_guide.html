Hood / Hurricane 4 Engine
Hood open — CENTERPIECE STOP
▼
Reframe Before You Start

"Before I tell you about this engine, I want to address the elephant in the room — because somebody is going to bring it up. Yes, the V8 is gone. Yes, this is a 2.0-liter four-cylinder. And yes, it makes more power than the V6 it replaced, more torque than the old Hemi at usable RPMs, and it runs on regular 87-octane. Let me show you why."

The Numbers First

"The Hurricane 4 produces 324 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque. The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 it replaces made 290 horsepower and 260 lb-ft. That's 34 more horsepower and 72 more pound-feet of torque out of an engine that's almost half the displacement. Towing capacity? Still 6,200 lbs — identical to the V6."

Power Density — The Real Story

"This engine produces 162 horsepower per liter — 26% more power density than a Hellcat Redeye V8. It's not about displacement anymore. It's about how efficiently you burn fuel. This is the same principle Formula 1 engineers use, now in a family SUV."

TJI — The Blowtorch Effect

"The secret is Turbulent Jet Ignition — TJI. Standard engines use one spark plug to start a slow flame front across the cylinder. The Hurricane 4 uses a tiny pre-chamber above each cylinder. When it ignites, it shoots high-energy jets of flame into the main chamber from eight different angles simultaneously — like a blowtorch instead of a match. Faster burn, cleaner combustion, more power from every drop of fuel. This was previously only found in Formula 1 cars and the Maserati Nettuno V6. Now it's in a family SUV at this dealership."

Torque Curve — The Driving Feel

"90 percent of peak torque available from 2,600 RPM all the way to 5,600 RPM. You're not waiting for the engine to wake up. The moment you press the accelerator to merge or pass, the response is instant."

Built to Last

"Cylinder walls are 24% thicker than previous 2.0-liter designs. The bore lining is aerospace-grade PTWA coating — steel alloy melted at over 4,000 degrees and sprayed onto the cylinder walls — ten times more wear-resistant than traditional iron. Electric water pump continues to circulate coolant after shutdown to protect the turbo bearings. Jeep ran this engine at wide-open throttle for hundreds of hours before production."

So What
Don't rush this stop. The Hurricane 4 is the biggest story of this refresh. Give TJI a full 60 seconds. If they understand the blowtorch analogy, the "small engine" objection is gone before the desk.
Handle at this stop
"A 4-cylinder in a 5,000-pound Jeep — I don't know about that."
"Totally fair reaction. 162 horsepower per liter — that's more power density than a Hellcat V8. It's not a small engine trying to keep up; it's a precision engine that outpunches the V6 it replaced at every point in the rev range. Jeep expects this engine to be 60-65% of all Grand Cherokee sales. That's not niche — it's the new standard."
